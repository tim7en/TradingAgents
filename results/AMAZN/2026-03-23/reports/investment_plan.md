Bear’s strongest points
- CapEx is huge and may not pay off quickly; PPE is large, FCF is lumpy, and shareholders are funding long, uncertain paybacks. That raises execution risk and downside if AI monetization or robotics ROI disappoints.
- Competitive and regulatory risk matter: hyperscale peers are aggressive on AI pricing and the EU “gatekeeper”/regulatory path could impose meaningful costs or force business model changes in Europe and elsewhere.
- Technicals are negative in the medium term: price below 10EMA/50SMA/200SMA, volume center (VWMA) above price, MACD still negative — sellers control the short/medium regime and downside can be quick (elevated ATR).
- Valuation already prices in substantial success (TTM PE ~29.5, forward ~22.6); a re‑rating to the high‑teens on execution or regulatory disappointment could produce 20–35% downside into the $140–170 zone.

My decision — clear and actionable recommendation

Recommendation: BUY for disciplined, staged long-term exposure; For short‑term traders or unhedged investors, adopt a controlled, conditional approach (partial entry now, add on confirmation; use hedges).

The bear makes a strong case to avoid full, unhedged exposure today — which we will heed — but those risks do not outweigh the structural upside optionality for a measured, risk‑aware buyer.
- Technicals favour caution, not capitulation. That argues for staging and hedging rather than an immediate, full‑size buy or an outright sell. The long‑term trend (200‑SMA slope) still supports a strategic buy decision; the medium term requires discipline.
